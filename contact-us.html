    What Are the Egyptian Visa Requirements?

    To visit Egypt, here are the visa requirements based on different options:

    Visa on Arrival (30 days):

    • You must be from one of the eligible countries.
    • Have a valid passport with at least 6 months remaining.
    • Pay $25 USD in cash upon arrival.

    E-Visa (30 days):

    • Have a valid passport with at least 8 months validity.
    • Complete the online application.
    • Pay the e-visa fee.
    • Print the e-visa to present to the airport border guard upon arrival.

    Free Visa (90 days):

    • Eligible travelers may obtain a free visa valid for 90 days. Specific eligibility criteria apply.

    Ensure you check the latest visa requirements before your trip to Egypt. For more details and assistance with visa applications, click here.

    What Are the Top Traditional Egyptian Foods?

    Egypt boasts a rich culinary heritage with several must-try dishes:

    • Ful and Ta’meya (Fava Beans and Falafel): A popular breakfast dish featuring seasoned fava beans and crispy falafel.
    • Mulukhiya: A nutritious green soup made from jute leaves, often served with rice or bread.
    • Koshary: A beloved Egyptian street food dish made with rice, macaroni, lentils, and topped with tomato sauce, caramelized onions, and chickpeas.
    • Kebab and Kofta: Grilled skewers of seasoned meat, usually served with rice or bread.

    Explore these traditional Egyptian dishes for a taste of the country’s culinary delights.

    What Are the Finest Destinations to Visit in Egypt?

    Egypt is a treasure trove of breathtaking destinations that deserve to be explored. Here are some must-see places:

    • Cairo: Marvel at the Pyramids of Giza, the Great Sphinx, the Egyptian Museum, and the bustling Khan El Khalili Bazaar.
    • Luxor: Explore the Valley of the Kings, the magnificent Karnak Temple, and the Hatshepsut Temple.
    • Aswan: Visit the iconic Abu Simbel Temples, the Philae Temple, the Unfinished Obelisk, and more.
    • Hurghada: Enjoy the stunning Red Sea beaches and underwater wonders.
    • Alexandria: Discover the historic Qaitbay Citadel, Pompey’s Pillar, and the famous Alexandria Library.

    These destinations offer a perfect blend of ancient history, vibrant culture, and natural beauty.

    Is It Safe to Travel to Egypt?

    Yes, you will feel safe traveling to Egypt. The current atmosphere in the country is quite peaceful. The government has taken powerful measures to ensure safety, including restructuring the entire tourist police force to cover all important and tourist attractions in Egypt.

    What Are Egypt's Festivals and Public Holidays?

    Egypt celebrates numerous public holidays, both religious and national. Some of the most important festivals include:

    • Ramadan: The holy month of fasting, reflection, and community, ending with the festive holiday of Eid al-Fitr.
    • Eid al-Fitr: A significant celebration marking the end of Ramadan, featuring feasts, family gatherings, and special prayers.
    • Christmas: Celebrated on January 7th by the Coptic Christian community.
    • New Year’s Eve: Marked with various celebrations across the country.
    What to Wear While in Egypt?

    Wear whatever feels right and comfortable. Here are some recommendations:

    • Clothing: Light, breathable fabrics to stay cool. Modest attire is advised, especially when visiting religious sites.
    • Footwear: Comfortable, closed-toe shoes to handle Egypt’s diverse terrain.
    • Sun Protection: Apply sunscreen and wear sunglasses and a hat to protect yourself from the sun, especially during the summer.
    What Are Special Advice for Foreign Women in Egypt?

    While Egypt is one of the more liberal Islamic countries, it has become more conservative in recent decades. Here are some tips for foreign women to ensure a comfortable and respectful experience:

    • Modest Clothing: Avoid showing your chest, shoulders, or legs below the knees.
    • Behavior: Be mindful of local customs and cultural sensitivities.
    • Safety: Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid traveling alone at night.

    What Are the Official Languages in Egypt?

    The official languages in Egypt include:

    • Arabic: The official language of Egypt.
    • English: Widely understood and spoken, especially in cities.
    • Other Languages: Some Egyptians also speak French, Italian, Spanish, and German, particularly those working in tourism.

    Professional tour guides in Egypt are well-equipped to assist visitors who do not speak Arabic, ensuring they can communicate effectively in English and other languages to meet all clients’ needs.

    What Are the Transportation Options in Egypt?

    Egypt offers several transportation options to help you get around efficiently:

    • Uber/Taxis: The fastest way to travel, especially by taxi. In Cairo, white taxis and Uber are convenient choices for quicker travel.
    • Metro: In Cairo, the metro is the fastest way to get around, especially during rush hour when roads are congested.
    What Is the Weather Like in Egypt?

    Egypt experiences a range of temperatures throughout the year:

    • Summer: Generally hot with temperatures ranging from 23°C to 37°C. Nights can occasionally be cooler.
    • Winter: Cool and mild with temperatures ranging from 9.5°C to 17°C.

    Average Temperatures:

    • Low: Varies from 9.5°C in winter to 23°C in summer.
    • High: Varies from 17°C in winter to 32°C in summer.

    Coastal Areas: Temperatures are more moderate along the coasts, making it a pleasant destination year-round.
